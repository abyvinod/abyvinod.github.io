Problem formulation

Real-world problems are typically stochasticity and high-dimensional. Stochasticity may arise from human actions, unmodeled phenomena (e.g. weather), and uncertainty in system parameters. As we strive for more automation, it is natural ask for guarantees of safety and performance, which is a hard problem. This project aims to provide scalable and tractable guarantees of safety and performance in real world problems, described as continuous-state discrete-time stochastic models.

For these models, this project tries to address the following problems:

  1. Forward stochastic reachability problem: For an uncontrolled stochastic system and a given initial state, what is:
    1. the set of possible values1 of the state in future?
    2. the likelihood associated with it?
  2. Backward stochastic reachability problem: For a stochastic system with bounded control authority and a set of (possibly time-varying) constraints on the state,
    1. What is the maximum likelihood of satisfying these constraints from a given initial state?
    2. How can we design a controller that achieves this computed likelihood while satisfying the specified control bounds?
    3. What is the set of initial states that can achieve a specified likelihood of safety?
  3. Stochastic optimal control with safety as a priority: Can we design controllers that maximize safety while simultaneously minimize an additional cost function, for example, fuel costs?

Solutions proposed

I have used Fourier transforms to answer Problem 1.

To answer Problem 2, I have proposed:

  1. a recursion-free, parallelizable, and anytime approach that relies on Fourier transforms, convex optimization, and gradient-free optimization techniques to propose an open-loop underapproximation of the probability, the controller synthesis, and the set, and
  2. (developed in collaboration with Joseph Gleason), a set operation-based approach to compute an underapproximation of the stochastic reach-avoid by solving a related robust reach-avoid problem.

I have used bicriterion optimization techniques to address Problem 3.

Awards and recognition

During my PhD research under the advisement of Dr. Meeko Oishi, I have developed scalable and grid-free techniques to answer these problems. This work has won multiple recognitions:

  1. Best student paper award at 2017 ACM Proceedings of Hybrid Systems: Computation and Control, Pittsburgh, USA.
  2. Finalist for the best paper award at 2018 ACM Proceedings of Hybrid Systems: Computation and Control, Porto, Portugal.

Future work

  1. I am building, in collaboration with Joseph Gleason, a MATLAB toolbox to perform stochastic reachability computations using the proposed methods.
  2. I am also extending these results to more richer classes of systems and control policies.
