Biography

Abraham P. Vinod received the B.Tech. and the M.Tech degree in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras in 2014. He is currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of New Mexico.

His research interests are in the areas of stochastic control and optimization, specifically in stochastic reachability analysis and ensuring efficient and safe human-automation interactions. He has developed an open-sourced MATLAB toolbox for stochastic reachability analysis (SReachTools) with J. Gleason.

His research has received various recognitions:

  • Best Student Paper Award in the 2017 ACM Hybrid Systems: Computation and Control Conference,
  • Best Paper Award finalist in the 2018 ACM Hybrid Systems: Computation and Control Conference, and
  • Prof. Achim Bopp Prize for his M. Tech thesis.

Scalable Underapproximative Verification of Stochastic LTI Systems Using Convexity and Compactness

Sufficient conditions for the convexity and compactness of the stochastic reach-avoid sets (set of safe initial states for stochastic systems under bounded control authority) for LTI systems | Scalable and tractable polytopic underapproximation with controller synthesis by using the “oracle” proposed in our previous CSSL 2017 paper | Sufficient conditions for bang-bang safe policies | Thumbnail shows the underapproximative verification of a Gaussian-perturbed double integrator which is required to stay within the unit-box for 10 time steps. (Finalist for the best paper award)

Forward Stochastic Reachability Analysis for Uncontrolled Linear Systems using Fourier Transforms

Fourier transform-based characterization of the probability density/measure of the state for an uncontrolled stochastic system with arbitrary continuous-valued disturbance at a future time of interest | Sufficient conditions for log-concavity of the probability measure | Applications to stochastic target capture problem (Best student paper award)
